Twilio WhatsApp Setup Guide
1
Create a Twilio Account
Sign up at twilio.com and verify your account. Note your Account SID and Auth Token from the Console Dashboard.
2
Activate WhatsApp Sandbox (for testing)
Go to Messaging > Try it out > Send a WhatsApp message in Twilio Console. Follow the instructions to join the sandbox by sending the join code from your WhatsApp to the sandbox number.
3
Make Your Server Publicly Accessible
Twilio needs to reach your server from the internet. If running locally, install ngrok and run:
ngrok http 8000
Copy the HTTPS URL (e.g. https://abc123.ngrok-free.app) and append /webhook/whatsapp to get your webhook URL. If deployed on a server, use your domain directly.
4
Configure the Webhook in Twilio
In the Twilio WhatsApp Sandbox settings, set the "WHEN A MESSAGE COMES IN" field to your webhook URL (e.g. https://abc123.ngrok-free.app/webhook/whatsapp). Method should be POST.
5
Enter Credentials Here
Paste your Account SID, Auth Token, and the Twilio WhatsApp number (sandbox or production) in the Configuration tab. Enable the integration and save.
6
Test the Connection
Click "Test Connection" to verify your Twilio credentials. Then send a WhatsApp message to your Twilio number — the AI bot will reply using the same behavior configured in your AI Settings.
7
Go to Production (optional)
To use a dedicated WhatsApp Business number, apply for a Twilio WhatsApp Sender in the Twilio Console. Once approved, update the number here and set the webhook URL in the sender configuration.
